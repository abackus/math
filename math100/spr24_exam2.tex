\begin{exer}
Give a power series expansion of 
$$\int \arctan x ~dx.$$
(Hint: the derivative of $\arctan x$ is $1/(1 + x^2)$.)
\end{exer}

$$\int \arctan x ~dx = \int \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n x^{2n + 1}}{2n + 1} ~dx = \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n \frac{x^{2n + 2}}{(2n + 1)(2n + 2)} + C.$$

\begin{exer}
Approximate
$$\int_0^1 \arctan ~dx$$
with an error smaller than $1/80$.
Explain why the error in your approximation is smaller than $1/80$.
You can leave the answer as a sum of fractions.
\end{exer}

From the above calculation, the series expansion is an alternating series, and
$$\int_0^1 \arctan x ~dx \approx \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3 \cdot 4} + \frac{1}{5 \cdot 6} - \frac{1}{7 \cdot 8} = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{12} + \frac{1}{30} - \frac{1}{56}.$$
We stopped here because the next term is $1/(9 \cdot 10) = 1/90$. 
So the error is bounded by $1/90$ (which is smaller than $1/80$) by the Alternating Series Test.
